About Kkuma Park

The person behind Smart Daily Blogs.

My name is Kkuma Park. I'm an independent writer and developer based in Seoul, South Korea. I've been writing code professionally for more than a decade, built a handful of small indie products, and along the way piled up a list of personal experiments, mistakes, and hard-won notes.

Smart Daily Blogs is where I publish those notes, cleaned up and organized into ten niche sites so each topic gets room to breathe.

Why ten blogs instead of one

I tried keeping one general lifestyle blog. It was a mess — readers looking for budget tips didn't want to scroll past recipes, and pet owners didn't care about credit scores. So I split each topic into its own site. Same writer, same rules, different focus:

  • SmartMoneyDaily — personal finance, debt, investing
  • HealthyLifeHub — health and wellness
  • FitnessDailyTips — strength and fat loss
  • TechSimplified — technology, explained simply
  • CookingMadeEasy — weeknight cooking
  • HomeFixGuide — DIY home repair
  • ParentingSimple — honest parenting
  • PetCarePro — dog and cat care
  • TravelBudgetPro — budget travel
  • CarBuyingGuide — car buying and used-car checks

How I write these blogs

Three rules for every post, across all ten sites:

  1. Write from experience, not summary. If I haven't actually done it, I don't pretend I have. If I'm quoting a study or a product I haven't tested, I say so.
  2. Show the numbers. When real data, costs, or tests make the answer clearer, I put them in.
  3. Update when I'm wrong. If I discover a post is inaccurate, I fix the post and leave a dated note.

I use AI tools (including ChatGPT) to help draft structure and outlines faster — but every published article is reviewed, edited, and fact-checked by me. The voice, the experiences, and the opinions are mine. See the Disclaimer for the full disclosure.

Why this site exists

Honest answer: I'm building a small digital-product business and these blogs are how I reach readers who might care about what I make (Notion templates and other tools). Ads from Google AdSense help pay for the time and hosting.

I keep the sites free to read. If you find something useful, the best thing you can do is share a post with someone it might help.
